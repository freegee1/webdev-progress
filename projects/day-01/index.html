How to start learning Calisthenics

Calisthenics is a form of exercise consisting of a variety of movements which exercise large muscle groups, such as standing, grasping, pushing, etc.

Exercises are often performed rhythmically and with minimal no equipment, as bodyweight exercises.

They are intended to increase body strength, body fitness, and flexibility, through movements such as pulling or pushing oneself up, bending, jumping, or swinging, using only one's body weight for resistance; usually performed in high-repetition sets to develop muscular endurance.

Key guidelines when doing calisthenics. (Ordered List)

  1. Warm-up: Start with a 5-10 minute warm-up to get your blood flowing and muscles ready.
    • Light jogging or brisk walking
    • Jumping jacks
    • Arm circles
  2. Basic Exercises: Begin with fundamental calisthenics exercises such as push-ups, squats, lunges, and planks. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions for each exercise.
  3. Progression: As you get stronger, gradually increase the difficulty of your workouts by adding more repetitions, sets, or trying more advanced variations of the exercises.
  4. Consistency: Aim to work out at least 3-4 times a week to see progress. Consistency is key in building strength and endurance.
  5. Rest and Recovery: Ensure you give your muscles time to recover by incorporating rest days into your routine. This helps prevent injury and promotes muscle growth.

Key calisthenics workouts (Unordered list)

  • Jumping Jacks
  • Mountain Climbers
  • Burpees
  • High Knees
  • Butt Kicks

Here are some terms you should understand in Calisthenics!

Repetitions (Reps)
The number of times you perform a specific exercise in one set.
Sets
A group of repetitions. For example, doing 10 push-ups is one set of 10 reps.
Rest Periods
The time you take to recover between sets. This can vary depending on your fitness level and the intensity of the workout.
Form
The correct way to perform an exercise to maximize effectiveness and minimize the risk of injury.
